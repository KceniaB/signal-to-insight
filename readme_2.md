Karageorghis & Priest (2012) — Journal of Sport & Exercise Psychology — 
    the foundational work on music-exercise synchrony, 
    showing ~7% oxygen efficiency gain and ~10% RPE reduction when tempo matches cadence
Styns et al. (2007) — 
    showed spontaneous tempo preference clusters around 120–140 BPM for moderate exercise
Stöggl & Sperlich (2014) — 
    polarized training intensity distribution (which informs the 5-zone model)
The cadence→BPM formula I used (stride length power law) 
    comes from Heiderscheit et al. (2011) in Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise

What I didn't cite properly: the specific BPM ranges per genre are approximate, crowd-sourced consensus from DJ/running communities (Jog.fm database, Runner's World tempo guides), not peer-reviewed. That should be transparent in the notebook.
